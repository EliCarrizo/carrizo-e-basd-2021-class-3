    Radius (from Latin radius , ray) was discovered in 1898 by Marie Skłodowska-Curie and her husband Piere Curie in a variety of uraninite from northern Bohemia . While studying the mineral, the Curies removed the uranium from it and found that the remaining material was still radioactive. They then produced a radioactive mixture made primarily of barium that gave off a bright red flame color and spectral lines that had not been previously documented. In 1910 the radius was isolated by Curie and Andre Debierne in their metalpure by electrolysis of a solution of pure radium chloride using a cathode of mercury and distilling in an atmosphere of hydrogen .
